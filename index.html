Process

Definition

Every design starts with a problem. Whether it comes from internal stakeholders or external customers, additional information must be gathered and an end result clearly defined.

Prototyping

By starting as low fidelity as possible, other non-designers can be recruited to join in the ideation process. Sketching and wireframing helps avoid the sunk cost fallacy, and allows multiple ideas to be explored in a short amount of time.

Iteration

Once initial paths of research are defined, the most promising concepts can be taken to a higher fidelity in order to further test and refine.

Delivery

When a design is ready for customers, they should be delivered with as much detail as possible. As features are delivered, their use should be monitored, and additional changes should be made based on feedback and use.

Prototyping

As part of a design update for our older product offering, I was tasked with creating a cohesive feel across our suite of products that wouldn't requiring a long development process. In translating the old "flat" design to our updated "semi-flat" design, I noticed that adding a background behind our existing UI elements created the desired level of cohesiveness. To that end, I created a background using SVGs that expanded on what we had on the newer product by adding an increased amount of responsiveness. I was able to pass this background, along with additional implementation information directly to the engeneers, leading to an extremely short development process.

  • Date: December, 2016
  • Deliverables: Codepen consisting of HTML, CSS, Additional Implementation Details

Informal User Testing

We were nearing a large release of our software, and were looking to ensure that the new functionality we added was intutive and bug free. I pushed for a collaborative afternoon of internal user testing, or "dogfooding" in order to foster understanding of the product across the organization while also acheving the goals stated above. I developed a test plan containing the most critical features of the new functionality, and created a simple Google Form to gather any comments or bugs that were found. After facilitating the testing, I documented the findings in a testing summary and delivered my findings to key stakeholders, resulting in a more robust product.

  • Date: July, 2015
  • Deliverables: Test Plan, Report Form, Testing Summary

User Research

The market indicated a need for a more simplified version of our current software offering. I conducted interviews with subject matter experts to help define who our ideal user for this product was. By listening in on business development calls, I was able to better understand this type of user, and the failures of our current offering. From there I created user personas, verified them with the subject matter experts, and simplified our current software's workflow at key points of confusion. Finally, I produced updated screen designs and used the personas to aid in story writing ensuing increased cohesion between the business and engeneering departments.

  • Date: February 2016
  • Deliverables: User Personas, Workflow Diagrams, Full Fidelity Designs, User Stories
